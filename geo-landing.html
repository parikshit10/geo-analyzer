QUESTIONS? HANDLED.

What is GEO and why does it matter? add

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of optimizing your content to be cited by AI-powered search engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Bing Copilot. Unlike traditional SEO which focuses on ranking links, GEO focuses on making your content the source that AI quotes. As AI search grows, sites without GEO optimization risk becoming invisible.

How is the GEO Score calculated? add

Your score is a weighted composite of five dimensions: Citability (25%), Structural Readability (20%), Authority Signals (20%), Technical Accessibility (20%), and Multi-Modal Richness (15%). Each dimension is scored 0–100 based on dozens of specific checks, from passage word-count optimization to AI crawler permissions in your robots.txt.

What is llms.txt and do I need one? add

llms.txt is an emerging standard — like robots.txt, but for AI engines. It tells LLM crawlers what content to prioritize, what to summarize, and what context matters. While not yet universally adopted, sites with a well-structured llms.txt are seeing measurably higher citation rates in early studies. Our analyzer checks for its presence and validates its format.

Is my data safe? add

Absolutely. We only fetch publicly available pages — the same content any AI crawler would see. We don't store your HTML beyond the analysis session, and all audit data is encrypted in transit and at rest. No login required for your first scan.
